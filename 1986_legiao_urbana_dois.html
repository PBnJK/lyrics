Dois (1986)

Legião Urbana

Dois (Two) is the second album by band Legião Urbana (Urban Legion.) With the enormous success of their first album, the band, and specifically its lead, Renato Russo, felt pressured to deliver an even better showing—"second record syndrome," as put by guitarist Dado Villa-Lobos. Still, Dois ended up being a hit, featuring some of the band's greatest hits and selling about one million copies.

Left to right: Dado Villa-Lobos (guitar,) Marcelo Bonfá (drummer,) Renato Russo (vocalist) and Renato Rocha (bassist)
