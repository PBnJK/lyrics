# Ventura (2003)
= Los Hermanos

<img src="assets/covers/2003_los_hermanos_ventura.jpg" width="400" alt="Ventura cover art, depicting a bird in flight" />

Ventura (Destiny, luck or circumstance) is a bit of an indie darling, being voted the 68th best Brazilian album by the Rolling Stone magazine, and 1st (!) best by an audience vote held by the Estadão newspaper (Clube da Esquina.) It was also the first Brazilian album to be fully leaked online before its release.

The album marks a consolidation of the album's sound, with the samba and MPB influences from their previous work now featured front-and-center alongside rock

<img src="assets/band_members/2005_los_hermanos.jpg" width="400" alt="Ventura cover art, depicting a bird in flight" />
= Los Hermanos in concert

Even with all this praise, however, the album—or better yet, the band as a whole—is perhaps as beloved as it is <i>reviled</i>. You'll hear the nickname "Loser Manos" (Loser Bros) tossed around fairly frequently. This is all to say that, if your Brazilian friends balk or laugh at you for mentioning or even liking this band:

<i>"I warned you!"</i>

(It's not that serious...)
