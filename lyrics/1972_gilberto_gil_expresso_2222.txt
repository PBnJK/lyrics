# Expresso 2222 (1972)
= Gilberto Gil

<img src="assets/covers/1972_gilberto_gil_expresso_2222.jpg" width="400" alt="Expresso 2222 cover art, depicting Gilberto Gil's baby son, Pedro Gil, wearing overalls." />
= The cover depicts Pedro Gil, one of Gilberto Gil's children

After being put under arrest for two months by the dictatorship, hopping between headquarters and jail cells, Gilberto Gil exiled himself to London in 1969 alongside fellow musician Caetano Veloso. The pair spent three years immersed in the bustling music scene of the metropolis, witnessing the rise of Reggae in the UK, watching Miles Davis performances, jamming with David Gilmour and being headliners in festivals featuring names such as The Doors, Joni Mitchell and Leonard Cohen.

Eventually, the dust sufficiently settled, the dynamic duo returned to Brazil in 1972, and brought gifts: Caetano Veloso released the album "Transa," considered by many to be his <i>magnum opus.</i> Gilberto Gil released "Expresso 2222" (Express 2222.) The name, paying homage to the train Gilberto Gil rode on to leave his hometown, says it all: London was nice, but Gil missed Brazil, and he's going to use all of those new ideas he picked up while away to let you know.
